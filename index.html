About Me

I am a PhD student in Communication at Purdue University, specializing in Media, Technology, and Society, having the priviledge to be advised by the wonderful, Prof. Jeremy Foote. My research approaches online platforms and digitality critically, exploring how platforms influence interactions and focusing on the interplay between algorithmic systems and sociality. I hold degrees (BSc, MSc) in physics and applied mathematics from the National Technical University of Athens (N.T.U.A.) and an MA in computational social science from the University of Chicago, focusing on sociology. Among other things, I have worked as a student researcher at the European Organization for Nuclear Research (CERN), visited Complexity Science Hub Vienna (CSH), and worked as a software engineer in industry. The Onassis Foundation Scholarship program supports my PhD work.

Oh, I'm also a member of the Community Data Science Collective (CDSC)

Feel free to reach out if you'd like to collaborate or discuss research ideas.
